<br> <h1>Week 1: Introduction</h1> <h2>Final Project Proposal</h2> <br> <div> <p>I originally wanted to create a neurofeedback device using an EEG sensor to detect brain wave patterns, and then feed these into a visual display using something like unity 3D that could give a user real time feedback on their brainwave patterns. This would give the user the ability to entrain their brain waves toward a more optimal state, which could be used to reduce stress, increase relaxation, or develop concentration. </p> <p> After consulting with teaching staff and searching for possible ways to implement this project, we came to the conclusion that independently creating an EEG scanner would present a challenge beyond the scope of the course, and that purchasing the components would be financially inadvisable. I therefore decided to change my project to one more reasonably implementable. </p> <p> I still wanted my project to focus on the use of biometric data, so I conducted further research on biosensors that would be less challenging to create. I landed on the idea of using an ECG/EKG reader that could detect the heart rate of the user, using the AD8232 chip. These signals would be easier to process and less prone to interference. I then want to wirelessly send these signals to an external audio/visual device that would create a real time digital representation of the user's heartbeat, in the form of lights, colors, or sounds. </p> <p> The purpose of this device would be to enhance the user's interoception, which is defined as the ability to sense the internal physiological state of the body and its organs. Studies have shown that underdeveloped or inaccurate interoceptive sensing is negatively correlated with psychological wellbeing, and that intentionally improving this skill can improve health outcomes. By amplifying the user's biosignals, they can develop greater awareness of their physiological and emotional states, leading to increased ability for self-regulation. </p> </div>
